Asha Noorbasha does Checkov tonight

The Cherry Orchard by Anton Chekhov

LOPAKHIN.
What's up with you, Dunyasha...?
DUNYASHA.
My hands are shaking. I shall faint.
LOPAKHIN.
You're too sensitive, Dunyasha. You dress just like a lady, and you do your hair like one too. You oughtn't. You should know your place.
EPIKHODOV.
[Enters with a bouquet. He wears a short jacket and brilliantly polished boots which squeak audibly. He drops the bouquet as he enters, then picks it up] The gardener sent these; says they're to go into the dining-room. [Gives the bouquet]
LOPAKHIN.
And you'll bring me some kvass.
DUNYASHA.
Very well.
[Exit.]
EPIKHODOV.
